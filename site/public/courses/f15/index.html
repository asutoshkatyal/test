Philosophy 100B: Theory of Knowledge

Location & Time

  • Location: Theater & Dance West, Room 1701
  • Time: Wednesday and Friday 2:00 PM–3:15 PM + Discussion
  • Discussion Sections:
    • Thursday 8:00–8:50 AM in Girvetz 1116
    • Thursday 11:00–11:50 AM in Phelps 1448
    • Thursday 5:00–5:50 PM in Phelps 2514
    • Thursday 6:00–6:50 PM in Phelps 1444

Description

Investigates fundamental questions surrounding the nature of human knowledge and human justification, such as: What do I know? What am I justified in believing? What is it to know something? What is it to hold a justified belief?

Instructor(s)

  • Michael Rescorla

Prerequisite(s)

One prior course in Philosophy; or Philosophy 3 (which may be taken concurrently).

Philosophy 100D: Philosophy of Mind

Location & Time

  • Location: Theater & Dance West, Room 2600
  • Time: Monday-Wednesday-Friday 1:00–1:50 PM + Discussion
  • Discussion Sections:
    • Wednesday 9:00–9:50 AM in North Hall 1111
    • Wednesday 3:00–3:50 PM in Girvetz 2124

Description

Discussion of some central questions about the mind: are people identical to their bodies? What is it to feel, believe, or desire something? What distinguishes intelligent thinking from a computer’s activities? What is the connection between language and thought?

Instructor(s)

  • Kevin Falvey

Prerequisite(s)

One prior course in philosophy; or Philosphy 3 (which may be taken concurrently).
